Projects

Nonnegative Matrix Factorization - Rice REU STAT-DATASCI 2023

Nonnegative Matrix Factorization (NMF) is a dimensionality reduction technique used in data analysis and machine learning. It aims to decompose a nonnegative matrix into two lower-rank nonnegative matrices. In this project, we implemented and compared multiple minimum-volume NMF algorithms in both synthetic data and practical data. Also, we proposed a new algorithm named Square-Root Minimum-Volume NMF inspired from the Square-Root Lasso problem. We applied the Majorization-Minimization idea, which is a popular paradigm for solving hard optimization problem. Our method improve the previous minimum-volume NMF methods in terms of choosing the optimal tunning parameter.
I worked on this Project with Dr. Eric C. Chi at the Rice Department of Statistics.
Here is our preprint paper: https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2309.13733

Our paper is accepcted for publication at Proceedings of the 2024 SIAM International Conference on Data Mining (SDM24)!

Geodesics Nets Construction

A geodesic net is a network that connects multiple points with the geodesics while ensuring that each additional point is " balanced " and stretched equally by its neighbors through those curves. In this project, we are fining new algorithm that can construct geodesic nets on different surfaces based on the idea of Steiner trees, since Steiner tree are satisfied to be geodesic nets. Our algorithm is inspired from the construction of Steiner Tree by bubble soap film, which is illustrated in the video below.
I am working on this project with Dr. Ken Richardson at TCU Department of Mathematics .
Please try our algorithm at https://github.com/ductoanng/BubbleGeodesicNet.
                       

GO2AI

GO2AI is a project working with AI and the game Go with the goal of optimizing playing. It is inspired from the advent of AlphaGo 2016. In this project, we are aiming to train the AI "slowly" to discover the similarities and differences between AI and human learning process. We are applying Grad-CAM, an explainable AI method, to have better the understanding how the neural network influences the policy of the AI agent in the decision-making process. I am in the team, including Blake Good, Harrison Leath, Shawn Fahimi and me, under supervision of Dr. Liran Ma and Dr. Ze-li Dou at TCU Department of Computer Science.
